Principles of Organic Farming

Introduction:

Organic farming is based on natural processes that maintain soil fertility, ecological balance, and biodiversity. It avoids harmful chemicals and focuses on sustainable growth. By following these values, we can build a healthier, more sustainable, and eco-friendly future.

Main Principles:

  • Health: Organic farming aims to sustain and enhance the health of soil, plants, animals, and humans. Healthy soil produces healthy food, which leads to a healthy life.
  • Ecology: It is based on living ecological systems and cycles. Farmers work with nature—using compost, crop rotation, and natural pest control—to maintain balance in the environment.
  • Fairness: This principle emphasizes fairness to all living things. It ensures fair relationships between farmers, consumers, and the environment, supporting social justice and equality.
  • Care: Organic farming should be managed responsibly and carefully to protect the health and well-being of current and future generations. It avoids risky technologies like GMOs and chemical pesticides.

Conclusion:

The principles of organic farming promote harmony between people and nature. By adopting these principles, we ensure sustainable agriculture, environmental protection, and healthier communities for the future.
